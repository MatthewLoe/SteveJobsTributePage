Steve Jobs

Timeline of Steve Job's Life with Apple

  • February 24, 1955: Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco. He is then given up by his birth parents Joanne Carole Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali, and adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1960: The Jobs family move to Mountain View, California.
  • 1968: Steve Jobs, aged 13, got his first job working at Hewlett-Packard in the summer after speaking directly with Bob Hewlett.
  • 1969: Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak, who was 5 years older and bonded over a love of electronics.
  • 1972: Jobs and Wozniak build and sell illegeal 'Blue Boxes' that allowed users to make free phone calls.
  • 1973: Jobs enrolls in Reed College, but later drops out after a semester and moving into a hippie commune. Jobs later takes a job working with Atari.
  • 1976: In March, Jobs and Wozniak show the first Apple 1 board at Homebrew Computer Club. On April 1st, Jobs, Wozniak and Ron Wayne incorporate as Apple Computer.
  • 1977: In April, Jobs and Wozniak show the Apple 2 at the West Coast Computer Fair.
  • 1980: December 12th, Apple Computer goes public, entering the stock market and making Jobs, an already successful man, very wealthy.
  • 1984: January 24th, Macintosh is introduced at an Apple shareholders' meeting.
  • 1985: September 17th, after a coup at Apple removed Jobs of executive responsiblity, he and other individuals from Apple founded NeXT.
  • 1986: January 30th, Jobs purchased the computer animation division of George Lucus's ILM and incorporates it as Pixar.
  • 1989: In April, as NeXT and Pixar grew, Jobs was named entrepenuer of the decade by Inc. Magazine.
  • 1991: In May, Pixar, now solely devoted to animation with no remaining hardware production, signed a contract with Disney to produce a feature length film. The film released several years later, was called 'Toy Story' and was a huge success, making Jobs a billionaire.
  • 1996: In December, Apple purchases the NeXT operating system and brings Jobs back onboard as an advisor.
  • 1997: In July, Apple reinstalls Jobs as CEO.
  • 2001: October 23rd, the first iPod is released.
  • 2003: Jobs is diagnosed with pancretic cancer in the fall and refuses traditional treatment, opting for alternate therapies, but finally opts for surgery in August.
  • 2007: On January 9th, Jobs introduces the iPhone, the product most associated with Apple today.
  • 2008: The iPhone 3G is introduced.
  • 2009: In April, after taking a medical leave of abscence, Jobs undergoes a liver transplant.
  • 2010: Steve Jobs announces the release of the first tablet, the iPad and the improved iPhone, the iPhone 4.
  • 2011: On August 24th, Jobs resigns from Apple due to his worsening health and handed the reigns to Tim Cook. On October 5th, Jobs dies at home surrounded by family.
